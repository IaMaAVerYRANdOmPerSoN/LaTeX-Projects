\section*{Problem Statement}
From the set \( \{1, 2, 3, \dots, 20\}, \) ten distinct numbers are chosen at random, forming a subset. Let $M$ be the largest element among the ten numbers.
Find the expected value of $M,$ and simplify your answer as much as possible.

\section*{Solution}

\[
\Pr(M=m)=\frac{\binom{m-1}{9}}{\binom{20}{10}}\qquad(m=10,\do ts,20),
\]
and hence
\[
\mathbb{E}[M]=\sum_{m=10}^{20}m\Pr(M=m)=\sum_{j=1}^{20}\Pr(M\ge j)
= \sum_{j=1}^{20}\Bigg(1-\frac{\binom{j-1}{10}}{\binom{20}{10}}\Bigg).
\]
Using the hockey-stick identity,
\[
\sum_{j=1}^{20}\binom{j-1}{10}=\sum_{k=0}^{19}\binom{k}{10}=\binom{20}{11},
\]
so
\[
\mathbb{E}[M]=20-\frac{\binom{20}{11}}{\binom{20}{10}}=20-\frac{10}{11}=\frac{210}{11}.
\]
